Who we are


Strategic Alliance Community Development Corporation (Strategic Alliance CDC) is a 501(c)(3) organization headquartered in Detroit, Michigan under the leadership of Jason Dixon as the Chief Executive Officer (CEO). We are a sustainable community development organization and fiber optic service provider. Our “ Route to a Smart City” initiative will bring 1G fiber to the home and 10G Fiber to businesses providing internet services in urban communities across the nation.

What we do?

Financial Literacy

Financial literacy & empowerment programs in budgeting, debt resolution, retirement planning and enterprise development.

Workforce Development

  • Develop a talent pool of qualified fiber optic technicians
  • Develop and deploy our fiber ring service in the community

Homeownership education

Importance of having a banking relationship, home down payment assistance programs, buying a home with subsidized vouchers and receive homeownership counseling.

Agriculture

Eliminate food deserts and source food from land grant 1890 HBCUs which strengthen research, extension and teaching in the food and agricultural sciences.

Fiber Optic Broadband Network

  • Employ our FTTH/FTTB open network fiber deployment model
  • Public-private partnership model

Expected Outcomes

  • Closing the digital divide and addressing the economic and social inequality aspects of what has been a complicated and important issue.
  • Developed a talent pool of qualified fiber optic technicians, starting with a living wage salary who will be able to design, install and troubleshoot/test fiber plants via the training of residents ages 14-26 for careers in fiber optic installation.
  • Develop a fiber ring around the municipality.
  • Participants in our financial literacy & empowerment programs will obtain critical financial skills and exhibit an aptitude in budgeting, debt resolution, retirement planning and enterprise development. In addition, participants will learn the importance of having a banking relationship, home down payment assistance programs, buying a home with subsidized vouchers and receive homeownership counseling.
  • Financial empowerment through home ownership participants will learn the importance of having a banking relationship, home down payment assistance programs, buying a home with subsidized vouchers and receive homeownership counseling.
  • This is an opportunity for communities, organizations and leaders not just to talk about why no-one is doing anything but to actually be involved in plans to transform a disenfranchised community into a sustainable smart city with self-sufficient viable residents.
